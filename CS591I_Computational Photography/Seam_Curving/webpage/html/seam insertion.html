Seam Insertion

 

Seam insertion is a way of enlarging example without changing the size of the major content in the image. It can be seen as a inversion of seam carving operator. Since we try to find the optimal seams in the predetermined directions (horizontal or vertical) and insert pixels with low energy.

In order to balance the original image content and the artificially inserted parts, if we plan to enlarge an image by k, we first find the first k seams for removal, duplicate them and insert these pixels just next to the pixels of the optimal seams.

And if we want to keep the property of content-aware of the algorithm, we cannot push things too far. We need to break the process into several steps. Each step does not enlarge the size of the image in more than a fraction of its size from the previous step in order to maintain the major content of the images.

The algorithm can work for both the color images and the gray-level images.
